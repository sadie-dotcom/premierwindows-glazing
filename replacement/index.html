Before you enquire

Replacing your windows: your questions answered

How much does window replacement cost in Perth?
Most replacement windows are $1,300–$3,000 each, supplied and installed, depending on size, style and glass. A typical 3-bedroom home is around $8,000–$15,000. Every home is different, so your quote is exact, itemised and free.
Can you replace my existing aluminium or timber windows?
Yes — it's the majority of what we do. We custom-make each unit to your existing openings, remove the old frames and reinstall, so there's no rebuilding. Old single glazed aluminium and ageing timber are both straightforward replacements for our team.
How disruptive is the installation?
Most homes are completed in 1–4 days by our own installers, never subcontractors. We remove the old frames, fit the new units, seal, finish and leave the site clean — our Hamilton Hill replacement was finished in four days with zero mess. As a guide, plan for around 10–14 weeks from order to installation while your windows are custom-made in Balcatta.
Will replacement windows actually reduce noise?
Yes. Double glazing with the right acoustic glass reduces outside noise by up to 74%, taking you from around Rw 23 dB (old single glazed aluminium) to Rw 33–45 dB. If you're near a main road, freeway or flight path, it's usually the first thing people notice.
Do I need strata or council approval to replace my windows?
In most strata properties you'll need written approval before replacing windows, even like-for-like. Because windows affect the building façade, the decision usually needs sign-off from the strata committee or body corporate. We'll provide the specifications you need to submit.
What Australian Standards do your replacement windows meet?
All our replacement windows and doors comply with AS 2047 (performance testing and installation) and AS 1288 (safety glass), meeting minimum structural, water and energy performance levels. Our Low-E, argon-filled double glazed units achieve 7-star NatHERS with a U-value of 1.55.
Can you retrofit double glazing into an older or character home?
Yes. Every unit is made to measure for your existing openings, and our timber-look finishes suit character and heritage homes where appearance matters — exactly what we did in Hamilton Hill. We'll advise on the right frame style and glass at the free measure.
How long will modern replacement windows last?
High-quality uPVC lasts 30–40 years or more with minimal maintenance — no painting, no rusting, no warping. That's typically longer than the aluminium frames you're replacing, with none of the upkeep timber demands.
